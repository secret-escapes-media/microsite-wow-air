Iceland packs an astonishing amount of natural beauty into its roughly 40,000 square miles. The remote part-Arctic, part-Atlantic isle at the meeting of Europe and America more than lives up to its appearances in myth, in Norse sagas, on a certain HBO fantasy series – its exquisite scenery truly borders on the fantastical. Fly WOW air to Reykjavik from 12 cities across the U.S., and discover all this for yourself. Become the hero of your own unwritten epic or saga as you become acquainted with the colorful capital city, then head into the country and experience the legendary landscape firsthand.

Soaring mountains, jagged fjords, sparkling waterfalls, black-sand beaches – you'll find all this and so much more in the Land of Fire and Ice. There's a reason that artists, photographers and Hollywood location scouts flock like Arctic seabirds to this rugged island nation: It's a veritable treasure trove of natural wonders, from volcanoes to glaciers to lagoons. Lace up your hiking boots, and trek through verdant meadows and fertile valleys, or experience one of the most iconic road trips in Europe, past rocky cliffs and lava fields, in search of the Northern Lights. There's something for everyone in magical, mythical Iceland.

- id: golden title: The Golden Circle highlights-title: Get in the loop description: >

"The streets are paved in gold" is how some people describe a place where you can strike it rich – a place that will leave you wealthier than when you arrived. In Iceland, you'll find such a place in the 190-mile Golden Circle, from capital-city Reykjavik all the way into the southern uplands and back. "Wealth" doesn't even begin to cover to this iconic road-trip route's remarkable collection of natural attractions, from a national park to a geothermal field to a 100-ft. waterfall. See them over a period of weeks, days – even a couple of hours, if you're short on time.

- id: reykjavik title: Reykjavik highlights-title: Discover the city sights description: >

If you thought Iceland's natural attractions were exciting, just wait till you see its vibrant, cosmopolitan capital. World-class art, architecture, music and nightlife are just a taste of what you'll find in the world's northernmost capital city, home to the Hallgrimskirkja church, Harpa concert hall and Listasafn Reykjavíkur art museum. Explore the city center on foot, or if you'd prefer to rent a bike, pop by one of the many WOW bike stands dotted across town. There's plenty of nature to be found on the outskirts of the Reykjavik Peninsula, should you like to switch things up, from beaches to caves to rock formations.
